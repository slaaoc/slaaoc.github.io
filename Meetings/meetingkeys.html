Meeting Format Definitions

The meeting format definitions below describe key characteristics of the meetings that establish the focus for participation.  The described format will typically only use 10 - 15 minutes.  The rest of the time is for sharing.  While attendees are typically encouraged to share how they relate to the readings, they are welcome to share on other topics.

Each meeting is free to choose its own format and how it applies to the way the meeting is run.  This is usually described at the beginning of the meeting by the meeting leader.

Most meetings have a certain amount of flexibility so that as many as possible can share.


Anorexic Focus

The meeting focuses on the avoidance of sex and love. This also includes social and emotional anorexia.

Book Study

The group reads a portion of the S.L.A.A book and then possibly discusses the reading as well.

Closed Meeting

A meeting open only to those having a desire to stop living out a pattern of sex and love addiction.

Intergroup

This is a committee of S.L.A.A members that provide services to a local geographic area.

LGBT

This Group is specifically for Lesbians, Gays, Bisexuals and Transgenders, to focus on the individual issues we face in Sex and Love Addiction. It is an open meeting - everyone is invited. Please be confortable with other sexual and gender identities if you plan to attend.

Literature Study


The group reads a portion of a recovery related piece of literature. For example, this may include the S.L.A.A text, a phamplet, or any other approved literature. They may also discuss the reading afterwards.

Men Only

Attendance is limited to men only.

Mixed Gender

Both men and women are welcome to attend.

Open Meeting

A meeting open to anyone who wants to find out more about recovery from sex and love addiction.

Sobriety Chips

Congratulatory tokens are given at these meetings for various lengths of recovery.

Speaker

A portion of the meeting time is devoted to one person sharing their experience, strength and hope as it relates to their recovery from Sex and Love Addiction.  This person is normally selected in advance.

Step Study

The group focuses on one of the Twelve Steps. They read from either the S.L.A.A book or another 12 Step text, and usually progress numerically from one to twelve.

Topic Discussion

A member from the group will pick a sharing theme for the meeting. Members may also choose to share on other issues as well.

Women Only

Attendance is limited to women only.
